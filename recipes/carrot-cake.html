Carrot Cake

The Most Incredible Carrot Cake You'll Ever Gonna Taste

This Carrot Cake recipe is moist, flavorful and ridiculously more-ish topped off with a tangy Cream Cheese icing making it the perfect accompaniment to this Carrot Cake. If you have the time to wait I recommend you bake it the day before you serve it to let the sponge relax and the flavours mature.

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 210g Plain Flour;
  • 3/4 tsp Bicarbonate of Soda;
  • 3/4 tsp Baking Powder;
  • Good pinch of Salt;
  • 1/2 tsp of Ground Cinnamon;
  • 1/4 tsp of Ground Nutmeg;
  • 125g Nuts (Mix of Walnuts and Pecans);
  • 225 ml/g Sunflower or Vegetable Oil;
  • 120g Caster Sugar;
  • 120g Light Brown Sugar;
  • 3 Eggs;
  • 175g Grated Carrots;

For the Icing:

  • 100g Softened Butter;
  • 150g Cream Cheese;
  • 480g Icing Sugar;

Steps:

  1. In a bowl add the flour, the bicarbonate of soda, the baking powder, the salt, the ground cinnamon, the ground nutmeg, and mix all together using a whisk. (For a fluffier cake it is recomemended to pass all the ingredients through a sieve);
  2. Then chop the nuts but not too small, you still wanto to feel them when you take a bite, and add them to the bowl with the dry ingredients;
  3. In the bowl of a stand mixer add both the caster and the light brown sugar, the eggs, and whisk them on the medium speed for 4-5 minutes, then, set the mixer on the slow speed and pour the oil slowly, once the mixture is well combined set the mixer back to the medium speed and whisk it for a minute or so and turn it off (if you don't have a stand mixer use a regular bowl, and a hand mixer instead);
  4. Add the grated carrots into the mixer bowl and give it a good stir with a whisk;
  5. Finally, add the dry ingredients third by third mixing well after each addition, you can use the mixer or a whisk. Don't mix the batter too much, just enough until you see no lumps of flour;
  6. Pour the batter into an 8 inches tin greased and floured;
  7. Take the batter into a preheated oven to 180ºC (or 350ºF), and bake it for 45-50 min or until you check it with a skewer, and it comes out clean;
  8. Then take the cake out of the oven and let it cool down while you prepare the icing;
  9. For the icing add into the mixer bowl the softened butter and whisk it for 4-5 minutes, it will become pale in color;
  10. Then add the cream cheese and whisk it until both of them are well combined;
  11. Finally, add the icing sugar and whisk it for 2-3 minutes or until you obtain a thick texture;
  12. You can cut the cake in half, fill it and then put the rest of the icing on top or just cover all the outside of the cake;
  13. To finish you can chop some more walnuts and pecans and sprinkle them all over the top of the cake;
