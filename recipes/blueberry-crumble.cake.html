Blueberry Crumble Cake

The Perfect Combination of Fluffiness and Crunchiness

This cake is simple to make but brings a wonderful complexity of textures to your mouth, it is a fluffy sponge with the juiciness of the blueberries in the middle and on top of that a crunch crumble, I highly recommend you try it, I bet it will become one of your favorite cakes as soon as you taste it.

Ingredients

For the Crumble:

  • 75g Melted Butter;
  • 150g Granulated Sugar;
  • 75g Plain Flour;
  • 1 1/2 tsp Ground Cinnamon;

For the Sponge:

  • 260g Caster Sugar;
  • 6 tbs Vegetable Oil;
  • 2 Egg;
  • 180ml Whole Milk;
  • 340g Plain Flour;
  • 3 tsp Baking Powder;
  • 1 tsp Salt;
  • 340g Blueberries

Steps

  1. First prepare the crumble; Melt the butter and put it aside to cool down a bit, then, in a bowl add the granulated sugar, the flour, and the ground cinnamon, mix everything together using a whisk. Finally add the melted butter and mix it using a spoon until you obtain a crumbling "dough";
  2. Using a stand mixer or a hand mixer, on the medium speed, whisk the sugar, the oil, and the egss for 4-5 minutos or until you obtain a voluminous and pale misture;
  3. Reduce the speed of the mixer to low and pour the milk slowly by the sides of the bowl; Turn back the mixer to the medium speed again, and whisk the mixture for 30 seconds or so;
  4. Passing through a sieve, add the flour, the baking powder, and the salt, whisk the batter just enough to disolve any lumps of flour;
  5. Finally, add the blueberries and fold it gently into the batter;
  6. Pour the batter into a 9 inches loose bottom tin (greased and lined with baking paper), then, sprinkle the crumble all over the top of the batter;
  7. Take it to a preheated oven to 180ºC (or 350ºF) and bake it for 45-55 minutes or until you ckeck it with a skewer and it comes out clean;
  8. If you'd like you can server it with some more blueberries aside and a splash of double cream (or heavy cream) on top;
