Lemon Drizzle Cake

A Classic to Have on Your Repertoire

This lemon loaf cake is quite simple to prepare but that doesn't make the experience of tasting it less rich, moist, and delicious, you should definitely give it a try and bake it today for your afternoon tea, you won't regret it, I promise.

Ingredients

For the Sponge:

  • 225g Softened and Unsalted Butter;
  • 225g Caster Sugar;
  • 4 Egss;
  • 225g Self Raising Flour;
  • Zests of 2 Lemons;

For the Drizzle:

  • 100g Caster Sugar;
  • Juice of 2 Lemons;

Steps

  1. Using a stand mixer or a hand mixer whisk the softened butter and the sugar for 4-5 minutes until you have a voluminous and pale cream;
  2. Then, add the eggs one by one whisking very well after each addition;
  3. Finally add the flour and the lemon zests and whisk the mixture just enough to disolve any flour lumps;
  4. Pour the mixture into a greased loaf tin (I recommend lining the tin with baking paper leaving some excess paper to facilitate when you take the cake out of the tin)
  5. In a preheated oven to 180ºC (or 350ºF) bake the batter for 45-55 minutes or until you check it with a skewer and it comes out clean;
  6. While the cake is cooling down for a bit prepare the drizzle, for that just mix the lemon juice with the sugar using a whisk until you obtain a thick-ish consistency;
  7. With the cake still warm stab the whole surface of it using a skewer and then pour the drizzle all over it;
  8. After 10-15 minutes the cake will have completely absorbed the drizzle, so you can take it out of the tin and serve it;
